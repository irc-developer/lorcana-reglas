# Doubles Events

> This article describes tournament or organized play procedures. It does not replace the Warhammer 40,000 Core Rules.

## Summary

A Doubles event has two players combine their armies into one force that fights another two-player force. Pairing and ranking operate by team.

## Scope

This article covers Doubles-specific army construction, force terminology, shared resources, and Core Rules changes. General event procedures still come from the Warhammer Event Companion.

## Army Preparation

- EPIC HERO units, datasheets limited to once per army, enhancements, and other once-per-army items cannot appear more than once across the team.
- An Upgrade can repeat within one player's army when normally permitted, but the same Upgrade cannot appear in both team armies.
- Select one CHARACTER model from either army as the team's WARLORD.
- Select one Force Disposition available to either army and record it on the roster.

## Force Types

- **Force:** both players' combined armies.
- **Unified Force:** both armies share all faction keywords. ADEPTUS ASTARTES armies do not need the same Chapter keyword.
- **Force of Convenience:** any force that is not unified.

Allied units are ignored when deciding whether the force is unified.

## Rules Changes

- In a Unified Force, models and units from both armies are friendly to each other. In a Force of Convenience, a teammate's units are neither friendly nor enemy unless they belong to your army.
- Each force gains 1 core CP, not each player, and cannot generate more than one extra CP per battle round.
- Both players share the force's CP. Stratagem usage limits apply to the force.
- A Unified Force uses shared army rules as one army. If both players chose the same detachment, it also uses that detachment's rules as one force.
- Otherwise, each army uses its own army rules, and detachment rules affect only enemy units and units in that player's own army.
- In a Unified Force, eligible units may embark in a teammate's TRANSPORT.
- Restrictions based on army size use each individual player's army points limit.

The players take their turn together as one player. Secondary Missions and VP belong to the team, and the team can receive 10 VP when its armies meet Battle Ready standard.

## Player Impact

Teammates must coordinate list uniqueness, Warlord choice, Force Disposition, CP use, Stratagem limits, and shared-rule effects before the event.

## Organizer Impact

Organizers validate restrictions across both rosters and rank and pair teams rather than individual players.
