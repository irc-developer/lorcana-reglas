# Dominatus Events

> This article describes tournament or organized play procedures. It does not replace the Warhammer 40,000 Core Rules.

## Summary

A Dominatus event combines the Dominatus campaign deck with the current Chapter Approved Mission Deck and adds event procedures for campaign phases, Agendas, Alliances, rewards, and pairings.

## Scope

This article covers Dominatus-specific organized-play changes. It does not reproduce the Dominatus deck or any battlefield layout.

## Event Guidance

- Each player must have a Dominatus deck.
- The event organizer is the Warmaster.
- At the start of each campaign phase, the Warmaster may choose that phase's Location card instead of rolling.
- The Warmaster may require a player to change Alliance.
- Acquire Relics, Discard, Fresh Supplies, and Test of Mettle timing moves to Determine Mission in the first battle of the next campaign phase.
- Auspicious Augury is resolved normally, with a recommendation that a player from another Alliance be present.

## Mission and Agenda Procedure

- Record one Force Disposition and one Alliance on the roster.
- Read the campaign phase's war zone rules aloud before the battle.
- If both players attempt Agendas, use the Alliance briefing comparison to determine the Agenda and its Primary Mission.
- If either player declines, each declining player uses the Primary Mission obtained from the Force Disposition comparison. A player attempting an Agenda cannot score that normal Primary Mission.
- During Declare Battle Formations, each player may assign up to three earned Upgrade cards, with no more than one Upgrade card per unit.

## Campaign Results

Players still use the normal event VP caps and Battle Ready score.

In campaign phases 1 and 2, the victor draws one Battle Honour card and the opponent draws one Battle Skill card. A draw gives each player one Battle Skill card. A player who achieved an Agenda draws one additional card of the same type and keeps one.

In campaign phase 3, the victor receives the Path to Conquest Battle Honour.

## Pairings

Players are paired against another Alliance. Beyond that restriction, pair by record, then win path, then randomly among equivalent players.

## Player Impact

Players need a Dominatus deck, a recorded Alliance and Force Disposition, and accurate tracking of Agendas, Upgrades, and campaign rewards.

## Organizer Impact

The Warmaster controls campaign-phase Location selection, Alliance corrections, and campaign timing. Those powers apply only to the Dominatus event.
