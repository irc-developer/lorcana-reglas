# Team Events

> This article describes tournament or organized play procedures. It does not replace the Warhammer 40,000 Core Rules.

## Summary

In a Teams event, each team member plays an individual game against a member of the opposing team. Individual results are converted into team match scores.

## Scope

This article covers team composition, matchup selection, Battle Points, Team Points, pairing, and ranking. Layout assignment within the matchup process is excluded.

## Team Preparation

- Each team has one captain responsible for timely list submission and end-of-round score submission.
- Only one player per team may use units with a given faction keyword, normally limiting each Codex to one player, including allied forces.
- The restriction does not apply where ally rules change faction keywords or where datasheets appear in multiple Codexes with different faction keywords.
- Each player records one available Force Disposition.
- A given Force Disposition can be selected by no more than the team size divided by five, rounded up. A five-player team therefore uses every disposition once; an eight-player team can use each disposition no more than twice.

## Matchup Procedure

The captain coordinates modules according to team size:

| Team size | Modules |
| --- | --- |
| 3 | Main Engagement |
| 4 | Main Engagement, Champion |
| 5 | Initial Skirmish, Main Engagement |
| 6 | Initial Skirmish, Main Engagement, Champion |
| 7 | Initial Skirmish twice, Main Engagement |
| 8 | Initial Skirmish twice, Main Engagement, Champion |

In an Initial Skirmish, each team secretly selects a Defender, reveals simultaneously, then secretly offers two Attackers. Each Defender chooses one opposing Attacker, creating two matchups.

The Main Engagement repeats that process and pairs the two refused Attackers against one another. The Champion module pairs the final unassigned player from each team.

## Team Scoring

Each player's VP differential converts to Battle Points (BP):

| VP difference | Winner BP | Opponent BP |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 0-5 | 10 | 10 |
| 6-10 | 11 | 9 |
| 11-15 | 12 | 8 |
| 16-20 | 13 | 7 |
| 21-25 | 14 | 6 |
| 26-30 | 15 | 5 |
| 31-35 | 16 | 4 |
| 36-40 | 17 | 3 |
| 41-45 | 18 | 2 |
| 46-50 | 19 | 1 |
| 51+ | 20 | 0 |

The two players' BP always total 20. A team wins the match only when its BP lead reaches the threshold:

| Team size | Required BP lead |
| --- | --- |
| 3 | 4 |
| 4 | 6 |
| 5 | 6 |
| 6 | 8 |
| 7 | 10 |
| 8 | 12 |

A match win awards 3 Team Points (TP), a loss awards 1 TP, and a draw awards 2 TP to each team.

## Pairing and Ranking Teams

Pair teams by Team Points, then total individual wins, then randomly. Rank tied teams by record, total individual wins, then total BP.

## Player Impact

Players must supply accurate individual VP and BP. Team-list restrictions apply even when each individual army would otherwise be legal.

## Organizer Impact

Organizers need tools for matchup recording, BP conversion, team thresholds, and TP standings. Captains are the primary submission contacts.
