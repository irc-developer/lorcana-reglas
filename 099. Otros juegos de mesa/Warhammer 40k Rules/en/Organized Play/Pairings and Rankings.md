# Pairings and Rankings

> This article describes tournament or organized play procedures. It does not replace the Warhammer 40,000 Core Rules.

## Summary

The recommended individual-event method pairs players by record and win path, while rankings use record, opponent win record, and finally total VP.

## Scope

This article covers general individual pairings and rankings. Team-match pairings and scores are defined in [[Team Events]]. The pack does not define byes, drops, or rematch avoidance.

## Practical Procedure

### Pairing Players

1. Pair the first round randomly.
2. In later rounds, group players by win-loss-draw record.
3. Within the same record, group by win path: the rounds in which wins and losses occurred.
4. Randomize among players still equal on both criteria.

Win path is intended to pair players with similar recent event experiences. It is not the same as total wins or formal strength of schedule.

### Ranking Players

Rank tied players by:

1. overall record;
2. opponents' win records;
3. total VP.

The companion advises against using VP as a pairing metric or an early ranking metric because score margins do not reliably show game difficulty. VP is a late tiebreaker.

## Player Impact

Players should expect later opponents with similar records and loss timing. A high VP total does not override a better record or opponent record.

## Organizer Impact

Organizers should keep pairing and ranking metrics distinct, publish any additional tiebreakers, and calculate opponent records consistently.
