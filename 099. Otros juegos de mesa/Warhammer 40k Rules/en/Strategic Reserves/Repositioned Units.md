# Repositioned Units

## Summary

[[Repositioned Units]] are units removed from the battlefield and placed into [[Strategic Reserves]] during the battle by another rule.

## Definition

Repositioned Units are units removed from the battlefield and placed in [[Strategic Reserves]] during the battle by another rule.

## Rules

If the reposition rule is used in the Movement Phase, it can affect a unit that has already moved that phase.

[[Repositioned Units]] retain whether they advanced, fell back, or disembarked earlier that turn.

Duration-based and circumstance-based rules continue to affect [[Repositioned Units]] while those rules remain applicable.

## Interactions

- [[Strategic Reserves]] provides the reserve state that repositioned units enter.
- [[Ingress Move]] is the default arrival move used when a repositioned unit returns to the battlefield.
- Some carry-over effects remain to be documented separately in the wiki.
- [[Strategic Reserves]] exempts [[Repositioned Units]] from the default destruction rule that applies to many undeployed reserve units at the end of the third battle round.

## Restrictions

- This article does not define a generic reposition procedure; another rule must be the source that removes the unit from the battlefield.
- State carried into [[Strategic Reserves]] is preserved when the source explicitly says the unit is repositioned rather than newly deployed.
