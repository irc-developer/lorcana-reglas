# Datasheets

## Summary

Datasheets are the unit-data surfaces that collect the main rules information used to field and resolve a unit.

## Definition

A unit uses a datasheet that presents the unit's identifying and rules-bearing data in one place.

## Rules

A datasheet contains the unit's identifying name and the structured information needed to use that unit.

That structured information includes profiles, abilities, weapons, unit composition and other rules, and wargear options when those surfaces are present.

The datasheet is the container for that information; it does not replace the separate canonical meaning of those child surfaces.

## Interactions

- [[Datasheet Name]] is the identifying title surface on the datasheet.
- [[Profiles]] provides the model and weapon profile data carried by the datasheet.
- [[Abilities]] is the umbrella rules concept for the abilities that can appear on datasheets.
- [[Weapons]] is the datasheet-owned section that lists the unit's weapons and routes readers into the wider weapon model.
- [[Unit Composition and Other Rules]] records composition and datasheet-specific rule text.
- [[Wargear Options]] records the selectable equipment changes available from the datasheet.
- [[Keyword System]] is adjacent because datasheets also carry the keywords that many downstream rules consume.

## Restrictions

- This article defines the datasheet as a container surface, not the full rules of every child field it contains.
- This article does not replace the separate meaning of weapon abilities, unit abilities, keywords, or profile characteristics.
- This article does not absorb codex-specific presentation or army-construction rules outside the current Core Rules scope.
