# Attack Sequence

## Summary

Attack Sequence is the ordered hit-to-damage pipeline that resolves each declared attack once weapons and targets have already been fixed.

## Definition

Each attack resolves Hit Rolls, Wound Rolls, Save Rolls, and Inflict Damage in that order.

## Rules

Attack Sequence is applied after attacks have been declared through [[Making Attacks]] and gathered through [[Resolve Attacks]].

Each attack first resolves [[Hit Rolls]].

If the attack continues, it then resolves [[Wound Rolls]].

If the attack scores a successful wound, it proceeds to [[Save Rolls]].

If the wound is not stopped, it proceeds to [[Inflict Damage]].

If an attack fails at one of these steps, or once it has inflicted its damage, the sequence ends for that attack.

Critical hits are still hits, and critical wounds are still wounds.

## Interactions

- [[Resolve Attacks]] determines which attacks enter the sequence and how they are grouped for resolution.
- [[Hit Rolls]], [[Wound Rolls]], [[Save Rolls]], and [[Inflict Damage]] are the direct child procedures of this concept.
- [[Mortal Wounds]] is adjacent to this pipeline because some attack effects add or convert direct wounds outside the normal save step.
- [[Attack System]] places this sequence inside the wider attack-resolution domain and its downstream consumers.

## Restrictions

- Attack Sequence begins only after weapons and targets have already been selected.
- The sequence does not itself decide what a model can attack or whether a weapon was legally selected.
- Rules that alter hit, wound, save, or damage handling modify this sequence only where their own text says they do.
