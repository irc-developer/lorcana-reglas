# Hazard Rolls

## Summary

Hazard Rolls is the procedure for resolving hazard tests that can cause direct mortal wounds to a unit when a hazardous rule or procedure requires them.

## Definition

To make a hazard roll for a unit, roll one D6. On 1-2, the roll fails and the unit suffers 1 mortal wound, or 3 mortal wounds if every model in the unit is a MONSTER or VEHICLE model.

## Rules

When a rule instructs a unit to make a hazard roll, roll one D6 for that unit.

On a result of 1 or 2, the hazard roll fails.

On a failed hazard roll, the unit suffers 1 mortal wound.

If every model in that unit is a MONSTER or VEHICLE model, the failed hazard roll instead causes 3 mortal wounds.

If a unit must make more than one hazard roll, those hazard rolls are made simultaneously.

## Interactions

- [[Mortal Wounds]] is the downstream damage rule used when a hazard roll fails.
- [[Fall-back Move]] is a published procedure that already connects to hazard-roll handling through fallback-related consequences.
- [[Attack System]] keeps hazard rolls adjacent to the attack pipeline because attack-related weapon abilities and other combat procedures can feed into this direct-damage rule.

## Restrictions

- Hazard rolls are made only when another rule instructs a unit to make them.
- Multiple hazard rolls for the same unit are made simultaneously rather than one after another.
- Hazard Rolls does not use the normal hit, wound, save, and damage sequence.
