# Ability System

## Overview

Ability System is the domain MOC for the rule-text classes that attach to units, weapons, and faction structures. It maps the core ability taxonomy, shows how this domain depends on datasheet structure and keyword handling, and points to the neighbouring systems that consume or constrain ability behaviour.

## Scope

This domain covers the ability taxonomy used to classify rules text across the repository, including the published umbrella article, the published rule-type family articles, and the published core-ability navigation surfaces.

It helps answer questions about what class of ability a rule belongs to, where a reader should look for ability-family distinctions, and when a lookup should move into datasheets, keywords, attached units, or reserves interactions instead of staying in classification.

## Domain Ownership

- Owner domain: Abilities.
- Publication role: organise the article family that classifies abilities and routes readers toward the correct subtype without redefining datasheet structure, keyword semantics, or downstream interactions.
- Domain system role: provide the authoritative entry point and navigation surface for the published ability-domain taxonomy and its consumer-facing routes.

## Domain Boundaries

Abilities owns:

- the ability-domain surfaces [[Abilities]], [[Rule Types]], [[Core Abilities]], [[Scouts]], [[Aura Abilities]], [[Faction Abilities]], [[Psychic Abilities]], and [[Wargear Abilities]].

Abilities does not own:

- [[Datasheet System]] ownership of the data containers that carry abilities.
- [[Weapon System]] ownership of individual weapon-side ability articles such as [[Close-quarters]], [[Devastating Wounds]], [[Indirect Fire]], and [[Precision]], even where [[Core Abilities]] indexes them.
- [[Keyword System]] ownership of keyword semantics even when abilities depend on keywords.
- [[Attached Unit System]] ownership of combined-unit-specific propagation and interaction rules.
- [[Strategic Reserves System]] ownership of reserve-state rules even when abilities create reserve-facing exceptions.
- combat, movement, or timing mechanics that abilities may modify downstream.

## Related Systems

- [[Core Concepts System]] provides the baseline terminology that ability articles will consume.
- [[Core Abilities]] provides the published attack-facing index for core and weapon abilities.
- [[Abilities]] defines the umbrella split between core abilities and weapon abilities.
- [[Rule Types]] defines the taxonomy split between aura, faction, psychic, and wargear abilities.
- [[Aura Abilities]], [[Faction Abilities]], [[Psychic Abilities]], and [[Wargear Abilities]] now provide the published subtype-family targets for that taxonomy.
- [[Scouts]] is the published core-ability owner for pre-battle scouting permissions.
- [[Datasheet System]] provides the upstream containers that carry ability text.
- [[Keyword System]] remains adjacent because ability classification and keyword semantics are separate governance problems.
- [[Attached Unit System]] consumes ability behaviour in combined-unit contexts.
- [[Strategic Reserves System]] consumes reserve-facing ability permissions and restrictions.

## Navigation

- Start here when the question is about what class of ability a rule belongs to rather than about a specific attack, move, or deployment procedure.
- Read [[Abilities]] when the question is about the umbrella distinction between core abilities and weapon abilities.
- Read [[Rule Types]] when the question is about aura, faction, psychic, or wargear classification.
- Read [[Aura Abilities]], [[Faction Abilities]], [[Psychic Abilities]], or [[Wargear Abilities]] when the lookup has narrowed to one rule-type family.
- Read [[Core Abilities]] when the question has narrowed to published attack-facing core or weapon abilities.
- Read [[Scouts]] when the question is about the core ability that grants pre-battle scouting options rather than about the movement procedure itself.
- Move to [[Datasheet System]] when the lookup is about where ability text lives on a unit's data model.
- Move to [[Keyword System]] when the lookup depends on keyword-qualified targeting or matching.
- Move to [[Attached Unit System]] when the lookup is about how abilities behave inside an attached unit.
- Move to [[Strategic Reserves System]] when the lookup concerns reserve-facing ability permissions or restrictions.
