# Battle-shock Rolls

## Summary

Battle-shock Rolls is the core Battle-shock rule that uses a Leadership roll to determine whether a unit becomes battle-shocked and applies the downstream state changes that other domains then consume.

## Definition

A battle-shock roll is a Leadership roll that determines whether a unit becomes or remains battle-shocked.

## Rules

On a successful battle-shock roll, the unit does not become battle-shocked.

On a failed battle-shock roll, the unit and each model in it are battle-shocked.

While a unit is battle-shocked, all models in that unit have OC -.

While a unit is battle-shocked, it cannot be targeted by its controlling player's stratagems.

While a unit is battle-shocked, it cannot start actions, and actions it has already started cannot be completed.

## Interactions

- [[Command Phase Battle-shock]] is the main timing surface that tells players when these rolls are made during the turn sequence.
- [[Battle-shocked]] is the resulting state applied on failure and removed when a later rule says the unit is no longer battle-shocked.
- [[Starting Strength and Half-strength]] provides one of the key conditions that determines which units must make these rolls during the Command phase.
- [[Objective Control System]] consumes the resulting OC - state, but objective-control calculation remains owned by Objectives.
- [[Stratagem System]] consumes the targeting restriction created by battle-shock, but stratagem rules and permissions remain owned by Stratagems.
- [[Actions]] consumes the action-start and action-completion restrictions created by battle-shock, but action rules remain owned by Objectives.

## Restrictions

- This article owns the battle-shock roll and the resulting battle-shocked state changes, not the downstream systems that consume those changes.
- This article does not redefine objective control, stratagem use, or action procedures.
- This article does not redefine Leadership as a foundational roll concept; it consumes that upstream concept.
