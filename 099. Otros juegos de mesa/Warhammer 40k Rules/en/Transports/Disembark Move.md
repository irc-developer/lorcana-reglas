# Disembark Move

## Summary

Disembark Move is the transport exit move used by an embarked unit during the active player's Movement phase, with Rapid, Tactical, and Combat disembark modes.

## Definition

A Disembark Move sets up an embarked unit from a TRANSPORT on the battlefield according to the selected disembark mode.

## Rules

The unit must be embarked within a TRANSPORT model that is on the battlefield.

The unit must not have embarked within that TRANSPORT this phase.

The TRANSPORT must not have made an advance or fall-back move this phase.

Rapid or Tactical Disembark uses a 3 inch set-up distance; Combat Disembark uses a 6 inch set-up distance.

Rapid Disembark is used if the TRANSPORT made a normal or ingress move this phase.

Tactical Disembark is used if the TRANSPORT remained stationary or has not yet been selected to move this phase, and the unit can be set up accordingly.

Combat Disembark is used otherwise and requires a hazard roll for each model in the unit.

Rapid Disembark prevents the unit from declaring a charge until the end of the turn.

Tactical Disembark then selects the unit to make a normal or advance move.

Combat Disembark makes the unit battle-shocked and prevents it from declaring a charge until the end of the turn.

## Interactions

- [[Set Up]] provides the general set-up framework.
- [[Ingress Move]] can constrain Rapid Disembark when the TRANSPORT arrived from reserves.
- [[Normal Move]] and [[Advance Move]] are the follow-up choices after Tactical Disembark.
- [[Hazard Rolls]] are used by Combat Disembark.

## Restrictions

- The unit cannot use this move if it embarked in the same phase.
- A TRANSPORT that advanced or fell back this phase prevents this disembark move.
- This article does not define emergency disembarking from a destroyed TRANSPORT.
