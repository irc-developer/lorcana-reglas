# Transport System

## Overview

Transport System is the primary entry point for the Transports domain. It maps the occupancy and capacity rules for transports, the embark and disembark branches, and the neighbouring systems that constrain entry, exit, reserve placement, and objective interaction.

## Scope

This domain covers transport occupancy, transport capacity, embark and disembark branches, and the planned glossary support needed to describe embarked status.

It helps answer questions about units entering, occupying, or exiting transports, and when a reader should move into movement, reserves, aircraft, or objective systems for adjacent mechanics.

## Domain Ownership

- Owner domain: Transports.
- Publication role: organise the rules for carrying units, embarking, disembarking, and resolving transport exit procedures.
- Domain system role: provide the authoritative entry point and navigation surface for planned transport-domain content.

## Domain Boundaries

Transports owns:

- the planned transport-domain surfaces Transports, Transport Capacity, Embarking, Disembarking, Disembark Move, Emergency Disembark Move, and Embarked.

Transports does not own:

- [[Movement System]] ownership of generic movement procedures used to embark or disembark.
- [[Strategic Reserves System]] ownership of reserve placement and arrival when embarked units interact with reserves.
- [[Objective Control System]] ownership of objective-control consequences after transport movement.
- [[Aircraft System]] ownership of aircraft-specific deployment and movement rules.
- [[Core Concepts System]] ownership of foundational unit terminology used by embarked units.
