# Destroyed

## Summary

Destroyed is the core-concepts rule that defines the destroyed state for models and units and the immediate consequences of being in that state.

## Definition

A model is destroyed when its remaining wounds are reduced to 0 or fewer.

A unit is destroyed when every model in it has been destroyed.

A destroyed model and a destroyed unit are subordinate states owned by this article.

## Rules

### Destroyed Model

A model becomes destroyed as soon as its remaining wounds are reduced to 0 or fewer.

Losing wounds is distinct from being destroyed: a model with one or more remaining wounds is not destroyed.

Rules that trigger when a model is destroyed are resolved before that model is removed.

If a model is destroyed by an attack, those destroyed-model triggers and that model's removal are delayed until the attacking unit has resolved all of its attacks unless a rule states otherwise.

### Destroyed Unit

A unit becomes destroyed when every model in it has been destroyed.

A unit is not destroyed merely because one or more of its models have been destroyed; until its last remaining model is destroyed, the unit still exists.

Destroying models in a unit can therefore change that unit's remaining model count, or a single-model unit's remaining wounds, before the unit itself is destroyed.

For a single-model unit, the model being destroyed and the unit being destroyed happen at the same time.

### Cause And State

Destroyed is a state, not a damage procedure.

Reducing a model's remaining wounds to 0 or fewer is one common cause of that state, but any rule that says a model or unit is destroyed causes that model or unit to enter the destroyed state.

This article defines when that state exists and its immediate consequences. It does not define the separate procedures that caused it.

### Immediate Consequences

Destroyed models and destroyed units cannot use abilities or be selected or targeted by rules unless otherwise stated.

When model destruction reduces a unit's remaining model count, or a single-model unit's remaining wounds, that can change later checks under [[Starting Strength and Half-strength]].

Models removed to regain coherency are destroyed, but they do not trigger rules that trigger when a model is destroyed.

## Interactions

- [[Units and Models]] defines the model/unit distinction that this article applies.
- [[Starting Strength and Half-strength]] uses the models or wounds remaining after destruction to evaluate strength states.

## Restrictions

- This article owns the definition of Destroyed, destroyed model, and destroyed unit only.
- This article does not restate Inflict Damage, [[Revived]], Deadly Demise, or [[Attacking Attached Units]].
- A model is not destroyed until its remaining wounds are 0 or fewer.
- A unit is not destroyed until every model in it has been destroyed.
- Models removed to regain coherency are destroyed, but they do not count as triggering destroyed-model rules.
- If a rule states an exception to the normal timing for destroyed-model triggers, removal, targeting, or ability use, that specific rule takes precedence.

## Frequently Confused With

- [[Starting Strength and Half-strength]], which tracks how many models or wounds remain after destruction but does not define when destruction occurs.
- Inflict Damage, which is one common cause of destruction when wounds are reduced to 0 or fewer, but does not define the destroyed state itself.
- [[Revived]], which returns destroyed models to a unit but does not redefine when models or units become destroyed.
- Deadly Demise, which is an ability that can trigger on a destroyed model and is not the definition of being destroyed.
- [[Attacking Attached Units]], which adds an attached-unit-specific destroyed-unit timing rule without redefining Destroyed generally.
