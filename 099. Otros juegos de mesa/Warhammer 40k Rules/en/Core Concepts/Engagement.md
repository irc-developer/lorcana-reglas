# Engagement

## Summary

Engagement is the foundational battlefield-state article that defines engagement range and the engaged or unengaged status consumed by movement, charge, shooting, and fight rules.

## Definition

A model's engagement range is within 2 inches horizontally and 5 inches vertically of it.

Models and units are engaged if a friendly model is within engagement range of one or more enemy models.

A unit with no engaged models is unengaged.

## Rules

Engagement range is measured from a model outward to determine whether enemy models are close enough to create engagement.

Engaged and unengaged are battlefield states derived from that engagement-range relationship.

This article owns the baseline meaning of engagement range, engaged, and unengaged.

## Interactions

- [[Set Up]] requires a unit to finish setup unengaged.
- [[Charge Move]] uses engagement as the successful endpoint for a completed charge.
- [[Fall-back Move]] consumes engagement as the state that a unit is leaving behind.
- [[Close-quarters Shooting]] consumes engagement as part of its permission model without redefining the state itself.
- [[Select Targets]] consumes engagement as one of the targeting restrictions that may apply.
- [[Fight System]] consumes the engagement state created earlier by movement and charge without owning the engagement concept itself.

## Restrictions

- This article does not define charge declaration, fight sequencing, pile-in move, consolidation move, or attack resolution.
- This article does not redefine the specific permissions or prohibitions that downstream rules apply to engaged or unengaged units.
- If a specific rule changes how engagement is checked or used, that downstream rule modifies the baseline defined here.
