# Coherency

## Summary

Coherency is the foundational formation rule for multi-model units, defining the horizontal and vertical spacing they must keep during setup and after moving.

## Definition

A multi-model unit is in coherency if every model is within 2 inches horizontally and 5 inches vertically of at least one other model in the unit, and within 9 inches horizontally and 5 inches vertically of every other model in the unit.

## Rules

Units must be in coherency when they are set up.

Units must end any kind of move in coherency.

At the end of the turn, a unit that is out of coherency loses models until coherency is restored.

Models removed to regain coherency are destroyed, but they do not trigger rules that trigger when a model is destroyed.

## Interactions

- [[Set Up]] requires units to be placed in coherency when they enter the battlefield.
- [[Movement System]] consumes coherency as a post-move legality requirement across generic move procedures.
- [[Revived]] consumes this rule when returned models must be placed back into their unit.
- [[Destroyed]] owns the destroyed state, but this article adds the exception that removed models do not trigger destroyed-model rules.
- [[Units and Models]] provides the unit and model structure that coherency applies to.

## Restrictions

- A multi-model unit that cannot finish setup or a move in coherency is not legally placed or moved.
- A unit that remains out of coherency at the end of the turn must lose models until coherency is restored.
- This article does not redefine movement paths, engagement, or reserve-arrival permissions.
