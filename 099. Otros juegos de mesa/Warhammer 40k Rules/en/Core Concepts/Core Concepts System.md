# Core Concepts System

## Overview

Core Concepts System is the primary entry point for the Core Concepts domain. It organizes the foundational articles that establish unit structure, strength-state tracking, and destruction-state transitions, and it points outward to the neighbouring domains that consume those concepts.

## Scope

This domain covers the foundational concepts that other rules use before they add phase logic, domain-specific procedures, or cross-domain exceptions.

It helps answer questions about baseline unit structure, strength-state tracking, destruction and revival state, and which neighbouring systems consume those concepts without taking ownership of them.

## Domain Ownership

- Owner domain: Core Concepts.
- Publication role: maintain the foundational concepts that other systems consume before applying their own timing, ability, keyword, or procedure logic.
- Domain system role: provide the authoritative entry point and navigation surface for published and planned Core Concepts content.

## Domain Boundaries

Core Concepts owns:

- [[Units and Models]], [[Starting Strength and Half-strength]], [[Destroyed]], [[Revived]], [[Measuring Distances]], [[Dice]], [[Set Up]], [[Coherency]], and [[Engagement]] as the published foundational articles in this domain.
- the planned core surfaces Army, Active Player and Opposing Player, Leadership Rolls, Active player, and Controlling player.

Core Concepts does not own:

- [[Attached Unit System]] ownership of combined-unit behaviour.
- [[Keyword System]] ownership of keyword semantics and mixed-keyword handling.
- [[Battle Sequence System]] ownership of turn and phase timing.
- phase-domain mechanics such as movement, charge, shooting, battle-shock, or fight procedures.
- [[Datasheet System]] ownership of the unit-data model printed on datasheets.

## Navigation

- Start here when the question is about foundational state or structure rather than a phase-specific procedure.
- Read [[Units and Models]] first for the baseline unit-to-model distinction.
- Read [[Measuring Distances]] when the question is about how distances are measured before a downstream system applies that measurement.
- Read [[Dice]] when the question is about shared D6 notation rather than a specific roll family.
- Read [[Set Up]], [[Coherency]], and [[Engagement]] for battlefield placement, formation, and engaged-state prerequisites consumed by many downstream systems.
- Read [[Starting Strength and Half-strength]] when the question is about tracking a unit's fixed reference values.
- Read [[Destroyed]] and [[Revived]] when the question is about state transitions affecting models or units.
- Move to [[Ability System]] when the lookup becomes about ability families, ability text, or how foundational concepts constrain abilities.
- Move to [[Attached Unit System]] when the lookup becomes specific to attached-unit formation or attached-unit interactions.
- Move to [[Keyword System]] when the lookup becomes about keyword behaviour rather than baseline unit identity or state.
- Move to [[Battle Sequence System]] when the question is primarily about timing windows or phase order rather than foundational concepts.
- Continue from [[Battle Sequence System]] into [[Battle Round]] or [[Turn Structure]] when the timing question needs a canonical owner instead of only the domain hub.
