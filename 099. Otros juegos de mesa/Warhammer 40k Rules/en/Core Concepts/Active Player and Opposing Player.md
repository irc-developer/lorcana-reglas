# Active Player and Opposing Player

## Summary

Active Player and Opposing Player defines the timing roles used to identify which player currently has action authority and which player is their opponent.

## Definition

At any given time, one player is the active player and the other is the opposing player.

## Rules

These roles change throughout the battle, but both players are always one or the other.

When neither player is taking a turn, the player who takes the first turn in that battle round is the active player.

During a player's turn, that player is normally the active player.

When a unit is selected to move, shoot, or fight, that unit's controlling player becomes the active player until that move or those attacks finish resolving.

## Interactions

- [[Battle Sequence System]] provides the timing spine where active-player checks happen.
- [[Turn Structure]] explains the normal turn context before the unit-selection exceptions apply.
- [[Controlling player]] explains ownership authority, which can temporarily determine active-player status during movement and attacks.

## Restrictions

- Active player is a timing role, not always the same thing as whose turn it is.
- Opposing player is the counterpart timing role and is not a separate ownership concept.
