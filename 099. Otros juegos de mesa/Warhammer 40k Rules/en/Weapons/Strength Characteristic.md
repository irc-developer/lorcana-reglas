# Strength Characteristic

## Summary

Strength Characteristic is the weapon value that gives the attack pipeline the offensive strength used when the target's toughness is tested at the wound stage.

## Definition

The Strength Characteristic is the S value on a weapon profile. It is the weapon-side value consumed when attacks reach wound resolution.

## Rules

Strength is recorded on the weapon profile and travels with that weapon into the attack system.

Its purpose is to provide the weapon-side value used when determining how hard the attack is to wound with.

Strength does not operate on its own; it is read in relation to the target's defensive profile at the wound stage.

Weapon abilities can change how wound handling works, but those rules still operate on attacks that originate from a weapon profile with a Strength value.

## Interactions

- [[Weapon Characteristics]] is the umbrella article that places Strength inside the broader weapon data model.
- [[Weapon Profile]] is the glossary surface where the S value appears with the rest of the weapon data.
- [[Wound Rolls]] is the downstream attack-resolution step that consumes the Strength Characteristic.
- [[Weapon Abilities]] covers profile-based rules that can alter wound handling or key off the weapon's characteristics.

## Restrictions

- Strength Characteristic does not determine whether the attack hits.
- Strength Characteristic does not determine armour saving throws or damage inflicted.
- This article does not restate the wound-roll procedure itself.
