# Weapon Abilities

## Summary

Weapon Abilities is the canonical article for the rules labels printed in weapon profiles that modify how a weapon can be selected or how its attacks behave once they enter the attack system.

## Definition

Weapon abilities are abilities listed in weapon profiles rather than in the datasheet Abilities section.

## Rules

Weapon abilities are carried by individual weapon profiles.

They modify only the weapon or attacks their own rules describe.

Keyword-qualified weapon abilities apply only against targets with the named keyword.

Multiple instances of the same core ability or weapon ability are not cumulative.

When duplicate instances exist, the controlling player chooses which instance applies.

Weapon abilities can modify different parts of attack handling, including weapon selection, attack gathering, hit handling, wound handling, save handling, or damage handling, but their full procedural effect remains defined by the specific ability articles or the attack-resolution pages that consume them.

## Interactions

- [[Weapon Characteristics]] places weapon abilities inside the broader weapon data model.
- [[Weapon Profile]] is the glossary surface where weapon abilities appear beside the profile's printed characteristics.
- [[Ability System]] is the neighbouring domain that classifies abilities generally and keeps datasheet abilities distinct from weapon-profile abilities.
- [[Attack System]] is the downstream system that receives the effects of weapon abilities once attacks are declared and resolved.
- [[Select Weapons]], [[Resolve Attacks]], and [[Attack Sequence]] are the main published attack-resolution surfaces that weapon abilities can modify.
- [[Keywords]] is a direct prerequisite when a weapon ability checks whether the target has a named keyword.

## Restrictions

- This article does not restate the full rules text of every individual weapon ability.
- Weapon abilities do not become cumulative merely because the same ability appears more than once.
- Weapon abilities modify only the stages and conditions specified by their own rules.
