# Damage Characteristic

## Summary

Damage Characteristic is the weapon value that tells the attack system how much wound loss a successful unsaved attack can inflict when damage is applied.

## Definition

The Damage Characteristic is the D value on a weapon profile. It determines how many wounds are lost when that weapon's attack inflicts damage unless another rule changes the amount.

## Rules

Damage is recorded on the weapon profile and remains attached to that weapon until the attack reaches damage resolution.

Its purpose is to supply the wound-loss amount for a successful attack that proceeds to damage application.

Some weapon abilities can change or reference the Damage Characteristic, but they do so as modifiers or consumers of the same profile value.

## Interactions

- [[Weapon Characteristics]] places Damage inside the broader weapon data model.
- [[Weapon Profile]] is the glossary surface where the D value appears with the rest of the weapon fields.
- [[Inflict Damage]] is the downstream attack-resolution step that consumes the Damage Characteristic.
- [[Weapon Abilities]] covers profile-based rules that can modify or reference the D value.
- [[Mortal Wounds]] remains an adjacent damage rule because some weapon abilities can route damage away from the normal save-based path while still caring about the weapon's D value.

## Restrictions

- Damage Characteristic is not the number of attacks a weapon makes.
- Damage Characteristic does not define the allocation or saving-throw procedure.
- This article does not restate how damage is inflicted or how mortal wounds are resolved.
