# Detachments

## Summary

Detachments are army-construction rule packages selected by spending Detachment Points before the game.

In the new system, an army can include more than one detachment if the combined Detachment Point cost fits the battle size.

## Definition

A detachment is a selected army-building package that gives rules to the army, usually shaping how the army plays and which units or themes it supports.

A detachment can also unlock one or more [[Force Dispositions]], which are mission-facing categories used later when determining the mission.

## Rules

Each detachment has a Detachment Point cost.

Detachments can provide rules that apply to the whole army, while some detachments or rules may only affect certain units within that army.

Detachments can provide enhancements, stratagems, and one or more Force Dispositions.

Current codex detachments remain usable in the new edition according to the source article, and new detachments are added to the system.

## Mixing Detachments

Detachments can be mixed if all normal army-construction requirements are met:

- the combined cost does not exceed the army's available [[Detachment Points]];
- the detachments are legal for the army being mustered;
- any restrictions, requirements, affected-unit limits, enhancement limits, and unit limits are respected.

The detachment's Force Disposition is not itself a mixing restriction. Taking a detachment associated with Purge the Foe, for example, does not prevent also taking a detachment associated with Disruption or Priority Assets, provided the army has enough Detachment Points.

The source explicitly warns that the system is not a return to unrestricted multi-codex soup armies. The army is still chosen from its Codex or legal army source; detachments then add rules that may apply broadly or only to certain units.

## Force Disposition vs Detachment Legality

A detachment's Force Disposition helps determine what mission orders the army can adopt. It does not decide whether another detachment can be included.

When several selected detachments unlock several Force Dispositions, the player chooses one available Force Disposition to represent the army for that battle. That chosen Force Disposition is what is compared against the opponent's chosen Force Disposition to determine the mission context.

## Interactions

- [[Detachment Points]] limit which detachments can be selected.
- [[Enhancement Limit]] constrains how many enhancement choices can be used.
- [[Upgrades]] modify how some non-Character units can receive enhancement-style benefits.
- [[Force Dispositions]] are granted by detachments and are used later to determine mission context.
- [[Stratagem System]] owns the actual use of stratagems during the battle.

## Restrictions

- Detachments do not reintroduce unrestricted multi-codex soup army construction by themselves.
- A Force Disposition category is not a legality cap on mixing detachments.
- This article does not reproduce faction-specific detachment text.
- The specific rules, costs, and affected units of a detachment need source-backed faction pages.
