# Force Dispositions

## Summary

Force Dispositions are mission-facing categories unlocked by detachments. One available Force Disposition is chosen to represent the army for a battle, and that chosen category is compared with the opponent's chosen category to determine the mission context.

## Definition

Force Dispositions are categories granted or unlocked by detachments.

A selected army can have access to more than one Force Disposition if its selected detachments unlock different ones. That does not mean all of them govern the mission at once. One available Force Disposition is chosen to represent the army for the battle.

## Rules

The source lists five Force Dispositions:

- Take and Hold
- Disruption
- Purge the Foe
- Priority Assets
- Reconnaissance

Each detachment gives one or more Force Dispositions.

When the army has multiple available Force Dispositions, the player chooses one of them to represent the army's Force Disposition for that battle. That chosen Force Disposition is compared with the opponent's chosen Force Disposition at the start of the battle to determine the specific mission details.

## Casual and Organised Play

In pickup or casual games, the source says a player can choose a different available Force Disposition ahead of each battle.

In tournament or organised play, the source says players will usually lock a single Force Disposition for the event when submitting their list, or otherwise follow the event pack.

## Not a Mixing Restriction

Force Disposition is not a legality cap on combining detachments. It does not mean the army can include only one detachment of a given category, nor does it prevent mixing different categories.

Detachment legality is controlled by [[Detachment Points]], army-source legality, and any specific restrictions on the detachments themselves.

## Interactions

- [[Detachments]] unlock Force Dispositions.
- [[Detachment Points]] controls which detachments can be selected, not which Force Disposition governs the mission.
- [[Army Construction]] determines the set of available Force Dispositions.
- [[Missions and Battle Preparation]] uses the chosen Force Disposition to determine the mission context.

## Restrictions

- This article records the source-backed concept and list of names.
- The chosen Force Disposition governs mission determination; the highest-cost detachment does not automatically govern.
- The number of detachments of each category does not automatically govern.
- The detailed mission procedure belongs to mission-specific sources and should not be invented here.
