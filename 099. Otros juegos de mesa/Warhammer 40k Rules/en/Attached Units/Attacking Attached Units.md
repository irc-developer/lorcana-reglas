# Attacking Attached Units

## Summary

Attacking Attached Units is the attack-resolution rule that determines which Toughness characteristic an [[Attached unit]] uses while bodyguard models remain and when destroyed-unit triggers resolve for that attached unit.

## Definition

When an [[Attached unit]] is attacked, use the highest T characteristic among its bodyguard models while any bodyguard models remain; otherwise use the highest T characteristic among the remaining leader and support models.

## Rules

While any bodyguard models remain in the [[Attached unit]], attacks against that unit use the highest T characteristic among those bodyguard models.

Once no bodyguard models remain, attacks against that attached unit use the highest T characteristic among the remaining leader and support models.

Rules that trigger when a unit is destroyed trigger only when the last model that started the battle in that attached unit is destroyed.

## Interactions

- [[Forming Attached Units]] defines how the [[Attached unit]] is created before these attack-resolution rules apply.
- Precision changes allocation behaviour against an attached unit without replacing this Toughness rule.
- [[Attached Unit System]] maps this article as the attack-resolution branch of the domain.

## Restrictions

- The bodyguard-based Toughness rule applies only while at least one bodyguard model remains in the [[Attached unit]].
- Destroyed-unit triggers do not resolve early when only part of the [[Attached unit]] has been destroyed.
