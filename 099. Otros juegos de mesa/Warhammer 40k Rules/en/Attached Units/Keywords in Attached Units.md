# Keywords in Attached Units

## Summary

Keywords in Attached Units is the keyword-behaviour rule that gives an [[Attached unit]] the combined keywords of its component units while leaving each model with only its own keywords.

## Definition

An [[Attached unit]] has all keywords of all component units, but individual models do not gain keywords they do not themselves have.

## Rules

An [[Attached unit]] has the combined keyword set of all of its component units.

Individual models inside that attached unit keep only the keywords they themselves have.

Because attacks target units, keyword-dependent rules check the full keyword set of the attached unit when they target it.

## Interactions

- [[Keywords]] is the upstream rule that defines keyword semantics and keyword-qualified targeting at the unit level.
- [[Forming Attached Units]] defines when the combined attached-unit keyword set comes into existence.
- [[Mixed Keywords in Units]] is the neighbouring rule for mixed-keyword behaviour outside the attached-unit-specific subtree.
- [[Abilities in Attached Units]] governs ability propagation and should not be treated as part of keyword inheritance.
- [[Attached Unit System]] maps this article as the keyword branch of the domain.

## Restrictions

- Individual models do not gain keywords that they do not themselves have.
- This article governs keyword behaviour in [[Attached unit]]s specifically, not every mixed-keyword unit configuration.
