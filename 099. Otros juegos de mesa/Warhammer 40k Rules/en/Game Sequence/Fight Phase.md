# Fight Phase

## Summary

Fight Phase is the turn-phase timing container for melee positioning, unit selection to fight, and melee attacks for both players.

## Definition

Fight Phase is the phase in which melee positioning and melee attacks are resolved for both players.

## Rules

Fight Phase resolves in this order:

1. Start of Fight Phase.
2. [[Pile-in Move]].
3. [[Fight]].
4. [[Consolidation Move]].
5. End of Fight Phase.

You must fight with all units that can fight.

Pile-in and consolidation are optional even for eligible units.

[[Fights First]] units are selected before non-Fights First units.

## Interactions

- [[Turn Structure]] is the parent timing article that places this phase inside each player turn.
- [[Fight System]] owns the melee procedures and sequencing that this phase contains.
- [[Fight]] is the canonical fight-domain article for unit selection and melee sequencing.
- [[Pile-in Move]] and [[Consolidation Move]] are the fight-owned positioning procedures bracketed by this phase.
- [[Fights First]] is the published state that changes priority inside this phase without replacing the larger fight procedure.

## Restrictions

- This article owns the phase timing, not the detailed melee procedures, movement branches, or attack-resolution steps used inside it.
- It does not redefine charge declaration, generic movement, hit rolls, wound rolls, save rolls, or damage resolution.
- Counteroffensive remains a downstream stratagem-side interruption rather than a Game Sequence-owned timing concept.
