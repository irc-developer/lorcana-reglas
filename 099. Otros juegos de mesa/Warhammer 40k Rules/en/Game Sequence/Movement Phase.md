# Movement Phase

## Summary

Movement Phase is the turn-phase timing container in which armies resolve movement-domain procedures, reserve arrivals, and transport-related movement windows.

## Definition

Movement Phase is the phase in which units are selected to move, including reserve and transport-related movement procedures.

## Rules

Movement Phase resolves in this order:

1. Start of Movement Phase.
2. Move Units.
3. End of Movement Phase.

Every unit in the army must be selected to move before the phase ends, including units in strategic reserves, even if they remain stationary.

## Interactions

- [[Turn Structure]] is the parent timing article that places this phase inside each player turn.
- [[Movement System]] owns the generic movement procedures used during this phase.
- [[Moving Units]] is the baseline movement-domain article that many movement-phase branches consume.
- [[Strategic Reserves System]] consumes this phase for standard reserve arrival timing and ingress-related interactions.
- [[Transport System]] consumes this phase for transport entry and exit procedures without taking ownership of the phase.
- [[Aircraft System]] consumes this phase for aircraft-specific timing windows and restrictions.

## Restrictions

- This article owns the phase timing, not the generic movement procedures that happen inside it.
- Move Units remains an unpublished structural node in the current source scope rather than a standalone published article.
- Reserve, transport, and aircraft mechanics remain owned by their adjacent domains even when they resolve in this phase.
