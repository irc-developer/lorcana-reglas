# Charge Phase

## Summary

Charge Phase is the turn-phase timing container for charge declaration, charge resolution, and the transition from positioning into melee opportunity.

## Definition

Charge Phase is the phase in which units resolve charge declarations, charge rolls, and charge moves.

## Rules

Charge Phase resolves in this order:

1. Start of Charge Phase.
2. [[Charge]].
3. End of Charge Phase.

Units must be on the battlefield, not engaged, within 12" of one or more enemy units, and must not have advanced or fallen back that turn unless another rule states otherwise.

## Interactions

- [[Turn Structure]] is the parent timing article that places this phase inside each player turn.
- [[Charge System]] owns the charge-domain mechanics that this phase contains.
- [[Charge]] is the published canonical article for declaration, charge-roll handling, and failed-charge outcome.
- [[Charge Move]] is the phase-specific movement procedure that executes a successful charge.
- [[Fight System]] consumes the melee opportunities created in this phase without owning the phase itself.
- [[Fights First]] is a downstream fight-order state that can be created by charging without moving ownership out of Fight.

## Restrictions

- This article owns the phase timing, not the detailed mechanics of charge movement or melee resolution.
- It does not redefine engagement, generic movement, attack resolution, or fight order.
- Phase-external permissions such as stratagem exceptions remain owned by their home domains.
