# Turn Structure

## Summary

Turn Structure is the canonical Game Sequence article for the ordered steps of a player's turn, routing readers from turn framing into the named phases without absorbing their downstream mechanics.

## Definition

Each player turn contains the operational sequence used to resolve the game.

## Rules

Each player turn resolves in this order:

1. Start of Turn step.
2. [[Command Phase]].
3. [[Movement Phase]].
4. [[Shooting Phase]].
5. [[Charge Phase]].
6. [[Fight Phase]].
7. End of Turn step.

Mission rules can resolve at end of turn after non-mission rules.

## Interactions

- [[Battle Round]] is the outer loop that contains each turn structure.
- [[Command Phase]], [[Movement Phase]], [[Shooting Phase]], [[Charge Phase]], and [[Fight Phase]] are the named phase articles owned by this same timing spine.
- [[Player Turns]] is the source-backed alias entry that resolves to this canonical article.

## Restrictions

- This concept does not redefine the mechanics executed inside the named phases.
- Start of Turn and End of Turn remain structural timing steps rather than separate published rules articles in the current source scope.
- Mission-owned end-of-turn resolution remains outside the ownership of this article even when it happens after the non-mission sequence.
