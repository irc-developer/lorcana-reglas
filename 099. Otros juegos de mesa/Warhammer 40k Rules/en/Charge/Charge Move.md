# Charge Move

## Summary

Charge Move is the Charge-owned movement procedure that converts a successful charge into battlefield positioning that can immediately feed melee engagement.

## Definition

A charging unit moves up to its charge-roll distance toward one or more declared [[Charge target|charge targets]].

## Rules

The move can use up to the distance set by the charge roll that was made as part of [[Charge]].

Charge targets must be within 12 inches and within the unit's maximum distance when the move is being resolved.

Each model that is moved must end closer to one or more charge targets.

If a moved model can end within 1 inch of a charge target, it must do so.

If a moved model can end engaged with a charge target, it must do so.

After the move, the unit must be engaged with all declared charge targets and must not be engaged with units that were not declared as charge targets.

Until the end of the turn, each model in the unit has [[Fights First]].

## Interactions

- [[Charge]] owns the declaration and charge-roll outcome that permit this procedure to happen.
- [[Charge target]] identifies the enemy units this move is required to close toward and end engaged with.
- [[Movement System]] remains the neighbouring system for generic movement framework and non-charge move types.
- [[Fight System]] consumes the engagement state and fight-order consequences created when this move finishes.
- [[Fights First]] is the downstream fight-owned state granted after this move is completed.

## Restrictions

- The move cannot exceed the maximum distance set by the charge roll.
- The move must respect the closer-to-target requirement for each moved model.
- If ending within 1 inch of a charge target is possible, the moved model must do so.
- The unit must finish engaged with all declared charge targets and cannot finish engaged with undeclared enemy units.
- This procedure does not redefine generic movement pathing, engagement range, pile-in move, consolidation move, or melee attack resolution.
