# Aircraft Movement

## Summary

Aircraft Movement is the aircraft-specific movement rule that restricts AIRCRAFT units to Ingress Moves and modifies how other units interact with them while moving.

## Definition

AIRCRAFT units can only make Ingress Moves and are placed back into Strategic Reserves at the end of the opponent's turn.

## Rules

AIRCRAFT units are not eligible to make any move type other than an Ingress Move.

At the end of the opponent's turn, every AIRCRAFT unit from your army that is on the battlefield must be placed in Strategic Reserves.

Models can move through AIRCRAFT models when making any type of move.

When making a pile-in, consolidation, or surge move, a unit that cannot FLY ignores AIRCRAFT units when selecting enemy units and determining the closest enemy unit.

Being engaged only with AIRCRAFT units does not stop a unit from being eligible to make a normal or advance move.

## Interactions

- [[Ingress Move]] is the only move type AIRCRAFT units can make.
- [[Strategic Reserves]] is the off-battlefield state AIRCRAFT units enter and re-enter.
- [[Flying Models]] matters because units that can FLY do not use one of the aircraft-ignore exceptions.
- [[Pile-in Move]], [[Consolidation Move]], and [[Surge Move]] are the movement types that ignore AIRCRAFT for non-FLY units.

## Restrictions

- This article does not make AIRCRAFT units equivalent to FLYING units for all purposes.
- This article does not override non-aircraft movement rules except where AIRCRAFT specifically creates an exception.
