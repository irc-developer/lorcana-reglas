# Terrain and Visibility

## Summary

Terrain and Visibility is the terrain-owned umbrella article that explains how battlefield features modify the default visibility state through cover, hidden state, obscuring, and solid blocking.

## Definition

Terrain affects visibility through [[Benefit of Cover]], [[Hidden]], [[Obscuring]], and [[Solid]].

## Rules

[[Visibility]] remains the default battlefield rule for line of sight.

Terrain adds separate but interacting layers on top of that baseline.

[[Benefit of Cover]] changes the defensive state of a target during ranged attacks.

[[Hidden]], [[Obscuring]], and [[Solid]] change whether models can be seen across or within terrain.

## Interactions

- [[Visibility]] provides the baseline line-of-sight rule that terrain modifies.
- [[Benefit of Cover]], [[Hidden]], [[Obscuring]], and [[Solid]] are the child terrain rules that carry the actual effects.
- [[Attack System]] consumes the resulting visibility and cover states when attacks are declared and resolved.
- [[Movement System]] remains adjacent because some terrain visibility concepts, especially [[Solid]], also create terrain-derived movement restrictions.

## Restrictions

- This article does not replace the child articles that define the exact cover, hidden, obscuring, or solid rules.
- This article does not define target selection procedure or the attack sequence.
- This article does not define how a move is performed through terrain; movement procedures remain in the Movement domain.
