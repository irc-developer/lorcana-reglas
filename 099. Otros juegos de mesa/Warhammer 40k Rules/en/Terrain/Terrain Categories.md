# Terrain Categories

## Summary

Terrain Categories classifies terrain features so that other terrain rules can determine which placement, visibility, and restriction effects apply.

## Definition

Terrain features belong to Exposed, Light, or Dense categories.

## Rules

Exposed terrain provides minimal protection and no meaningful hindrance.

Light terrain can provide cover and helps create obscuring terrain areas.

Dense terrain is the major visibility and restriction category used by terrain rules such as hidden state and solid terrain.

Features within the same [[Terrain area]] can belong to different categories.

## Interactions

- [[Placing Terrain]] establishes the terrain features that receive a category.
- [[Exposed]] and [[Light]] are published category articles within this domain.
- [[Hidden]], [[Obscuring]], and [[Solid]] consume category information to produce terrain-derived battlefield states and restrictions.

## Restrictions

- Terrain Categories classifies terrain; it does not itself define how a move is made or how an attack is resolved.
- Category membership alone does not replace the child rules that explain cover, hidden state, obscuring, or solid blocking.
- Dense remains a source-backed category name here even though it is not published as a separate article in the current roadmap.
