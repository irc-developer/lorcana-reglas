# Solid

## Summary

Solid is the terrain rule that blocks line of sight through enclosed ground-level gaps in dense terrain and also creates a terrain-derived restriction on where a model can end a move.

## Definition

Dense terrain features are Solid: line of sight cannot be drawn across enclosed gaps in the terrain surface that are 3 inches or less from ground level.

## Rules

This blocks visibility through small openings such as doors, windows, bullet holes, and small gaps between adjacent terrain features.

Models also cannot end moves such that any part of them is through enclosed parts of a Solid terrain feature 3 inches or less from ground level.

Solid therefore contributes both a visibility blocker and a terrain-origin end-position restriction.

## Interactions

- [[Terrain and Visibility]] is the parent terrain umbrella for Solid.
- [[Visibility]] is the baseline line-of-sight rule that Solid narrows.
- [[Ending a Move on Terrain]] is the Movement-domain procedure that consumes the terrain-derived end-position restriction created here.
- [[Movement System]] owns the movement procedure, while Terrain owns the Solid restriction itself.

## Restrictions

- Solid applies to dense terrain features and to enclosed gaps that are 3 inches or less from ground level.
- This article does not define how a move is performed, only the terrain restriction that can make an end position illegal.
- This article does not redefine attack procedure beyond establishing whether line of sight can be drawn.
