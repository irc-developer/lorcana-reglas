# Obscuring

## Summary

Obscuring is the terrain rule that turns qualifying terrain areas into visibility blockers when every line of sight between two models crosses them.

## Definition

Terrain areas containing one or more light or dense terrain features are [[Obscuring terrain area|obscuring terrain areas]].

## Rules

If every line of sight between two models crosses one or more obscuring terrain areas, excluding obscuring terrain areas one or both models are within, the models are not visible to each other.

Obscuring depends on terrain area composition and on the exact path of line of sight between the models.

This rule changes battlefield visibility state; it does not itself perform target selection or attack resolution.

## Interactions

- [[Terrain and Visibility]] is the parent terrain umbrella for obscuring.
- [[Obscuring terrain area]] and [[Terrain area]] provide the terrain-side support terms used by the rule.
- [[Light]] helps create obscuring terrain areas.
- [[Benefit of Cover]] can interact with the same terrain states, but it is a separate defensive outcome rather than the visibility-blocking rule itself.

## Restrictions

- Obscuring only blocks visibility when every line of sight crosses one or more qualifying obscuring terrain areas.
- Terrain areas one or both models are within are excluded from that crossing test.
- This article does not resolve target selection, shooting permission, or attack sequence steps.
