# Grey Knights Detachments

## Summary

Grey Knights Detachments is the navigation surface for the Grey Knights faction-pack detachments.

The pack introduces four faction-specific detachments, each centred on a particular Grey Knights battlefield role: Paladins, Purgation Squads, Interceptor Squads, and Purifier-led hallowed-ground control.

## Detachments in the Pack

| Detachment | Primary unit focus | Main rules hook |
| --- | --- | --- |
| [[Argent Assault]] | Paladin Squad | Melee efficiency against tougher targets, mortal-wound defence, Precision, Hazardous retaliation. |
| [[Fires of Purgation]] | Purgation Squad | Battle-shock pressure, improved snap shooting, stronger ranged attacks, mortal wounds into battle-shocked targets. |
| [[Immaterial Interdiction]] | Interceptor Squad | Post-shooting mobility, first-turn ingress, Stealth, action-and-shoot, reactive movement. |
| [[Warpbane Task Force]] | Purifier Squad and Grey Knights Infantry | [[Hallowed Ground]], hit re-rolls, wound re-rolls, defensive and positional stratagems. |

## Mixing Grey Knights Detachments

Grey Knights detachments can be mixed if the combined Detachment Point cost fits the battle size and all army-construction requirements are met.

At Strike Force size, with 3DP available, examples include:

| Combination | Legal? | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| [[Warpbane Task Force]] | Yes | 3DP total; only Purge the Foe is available from this combination. |
| Brotherhood Strike + [[Argent Assault]] | Yes | 2DP + 1DP; both are Purge the Foe. |
| Sanctic Spearhead + [[Immaterial Interdiction]] | Yes | 2DP + 1DP; unlocks Priority Assets. |
| Banishers + [[Fires of Purgation]] | Yes | 2DP + 1DP; both are Disruption. |
| [[Argent Assault]] + [[Fires of Purgation]] + [[Immaterial Interdiction]] | Yes | 1DP + 1DP + 1DP; unlocks Purge the Foe, Disruption, and Priority Assets. |
| Brotherhood Strike + Banishers | No at Strike Force | 2DP + 2DP exceeds 3DP. |
| [[Warpbane Task Force]] + [[Argent Assault]] | No at Strike Force | 3DP + 1DP exceeds 3DP. |

Force Disposition is not itself a restriction on mixing. The practical question is whether the list includes units that benefit from the chosen detachments: [[Argent Assault]] is focused on Paladin Squads, [[Fires of Purgation]] on Purgation Squads, and [[Immaterial Interdiction]] on Interceptor Squads.

## Mission Selection

If a Grey Knights army unlocks multiple Force Dispositions through its chosen detachments, one available Force Disposition is chosen to represent the army for the battle. That chosen Force Disposition, not the highest-cost detachment and not the number of detachments of each type, is what is compared against the opponent's chosen Force Disposition to determine the mission context.

In casual games this choice can usually be made ahead of each battle. In organised play, follow the event pack; sources indicate that tournaments usually lock a single Force Disposition when the list is submitted.
