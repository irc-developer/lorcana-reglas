# Close-quarters Shooting

## Summary

Close-quarters Shooting is the engaged-unit shooting mode that allows eligible units to make ranged attacks while engaged.

## Definition

An engaged unit that did not advance and has one or more CLOSE-QUARTERS weapons, or is a MONSTER or VEHICLE unit, may shoot while engaged.

## Rules

Close-quarters Shooting is an eligible shooting type used when a unit is selected to [[Shoot]].

MONSTER and VEHICLE models can target units their unit is engaged with, but most such attacks take a -1 penalty to hit unless they are CLOSE-QUARTERS attacks made against engaged targets.

BLAST weapons still cannot target units the attacker is engaged with.

Models that are neither MONSTER nor VEHICLE can only use CLOSE-QUARTERS weapons and can only target enemy units engaged with their unit.

## Interactions

- [[Shoot]] is the parent procedure that consumes this permission when a unit is selected to shoot.
- [[Shooting at Engaged Monsters and Vehicles]] defines the separate target-legality exception for enemy engaged MONSTER and VEHICLE units.
- [[Weapon System]] is the published neighbouring domain that owns weapon-side data such as whether a weapon is CLOSE-QUARTERS.
- [[Attack System]] supplies the shared attack pipeline once this shooting mode makes the attacks legal.
- This article consumes engagement state, weapon-side permission, and targeting restrictions that are owned elsewhere and therefore are not redefined here.

## Restrictions

- The unit must be engaged.
- The unit must not have advanced this turn.
- Non-MONSTER, non-VEHICLE models can only fire CLOSE-QUARTERS weapons through this mode.
- This article does not redefine engagement, weapon abilities, or the shared attack sequence.
