# Indirect Shooting

## Summary

Indirect Shooting is the shooting mode that lets an eligible unengaged unit fire INDIRECT FIRE weapons at targets that are not visible to the attacking model.

## Definition

An unengaged unit that did not advance and has one or more INDIRECT FIRE weapons may shoot indirectly.

## Rules

Indirect Shooting is an eligible shooting type used when a unit is selected to [[Shoot]].

INDIRECT FIRE weapons can target units that are not visible to the attacking model.

The target has the [[Benefit of Cover]].

Hit rolls cannot be re-rolled.

Unmodified hit rolls of 1-5 fail; this improves to 1-3 fail if the unit [[Remain Stationary|remained stationary]] and the target is visible to one or more friendly units.

## Interactions

- [[Shoot]] is the parent procedure that applies this shooting mode.
- [[Terrain Visibility System]] supplies the visibility states that this mode partially bypasses.
- [[Benefit of Cover]] is granted to the target as part of this rule.
- [[Remain Stationary]] is part of the condition that improves the hit-roll failure threshold.
- [[Weapon System]] is the published neighbouring domain that owns weapon-side data such as whether a weapon has INDIRECT FIRE.

## Restrictions

- The unit must be unengaged.
- The unit must not have advanced this turn.
- Only INDIRECT FIRE weapons can use this shooting mode.
- Hit rolls made through this shooting mode cannot be re-rolled.
- This article does not redefine cover, visibility, or weapon characteristics.
