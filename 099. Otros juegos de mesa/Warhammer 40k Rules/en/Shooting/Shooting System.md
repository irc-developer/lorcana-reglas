# Shooting System

## Overview

Shooting System is the authoritative System Article for the Shooting domain. It maps the published ranged-combat permissions, targeting exceptions, and special shooting procedures that sit on top of the general attack pipeline.

## Scope

This domain covers phase-specific shooting permissions, ranged target-legality exceptions, and special shooting procedures used when units resolve ranged attacks.

Readers should expect this domain to answer which shooting mode a unit may use, what phase-specific restrictions apply, and which neighbouring systems provide the data or shared procedures that shooting consumes.

## Domain Ownership

- Owner domain: Shooting.
- Domain role: publish and maintain the canonical surfaces for shooting permissions, ranged-combat mode selection, and Shooting-specific targeting restrictions.
- Publication boundary: this page organises the domain and routes readers to child surfaces; it does not replace the canonical articles for attack resolution, weapon data, terrain visibility, or movement procedures.

## Domain Boundaries

- Shooting owns phase-specific ranged-combat permissions, target-selection constraints unique to shooting, and special shooting procedures.
- [[Weapon System]] provides weapon data and weapon-side permissions such as the presence of relevant weapon abilities.
- [[Attack System]] provides the shared combat pipeline used after shooting becomes legal.
- [[Terrain Visibility System]] provides visibility, cover, and other terrain-derived states consumed by shooting.
- [[Movement System]] provides movement states such as having advanced or remained stationary.
- [[Battle Sequence System]] provides the phase timeline in which shooting occurs.
- [[Ability System]] remains adjacent because ability taxonomy and publication boundaries sit outside the Shooting domain even when abilities enable or modify shooting.

## Navigation

- Start here when the question is whether a unit can shoot and which shooting mode applies.
- Read [[Shoot]] first when the issue is the unit-selection procedure for resolving shooting.
- Read [[Normal Shooting]] for the baseline unengaged, non-advanced mode.
- Read [[Assault Shooting]] when the unit advanced and may still shoot only through ASSAULT weapons.
- Read [[Close-quarters Shooting]] when the unit is engaged and may still shoot through the close-quarters exception.
- Read [[Indirect Shooting]] when the unit is using INDIRECT FIRE weapons and visibility is the deciding constraint.
- Read [[Shooting at Engaged Monsters and Vehicles]] when the question is whether an enemy engaged MONSTER or VEHICLE can be targeted.
- Read [[Snap Shooting]] when another rule instructs a unit to shoot through the snap-shooting procedure.
- Move to [[Shooting Phase]] when the issue is whether ranged attacks are currently available in the turn.
