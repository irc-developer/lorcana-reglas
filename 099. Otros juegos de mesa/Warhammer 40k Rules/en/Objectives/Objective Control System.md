# Objective Control System

## Overview

Objective Control System is the authoritative System Article for the Objectives domain. It organises the published objective-control surfaces, the glossary support for objective interactions, and the ownership boundaries that keep battlefield state, control calculation, and mission interaction distinct.

## Scope

This domain covers how objectives are represented on the battlefield, how players establish control of them, how control can persist after units leave range, and how objective-based battlefield tasks interact with that control model.

This domain helps answer questions about what an objective is in play, how a player controls it, how that control changes, and which systems consume or modify those outcomes.

## Domain Ownership

- Owner domain: Objectives.
- Domain role: publish and maintain the canonical concepts for objective representation, objective control, secured objectives, objective-marker handling, and objective-based mission interaction.
- Publication boundary: this page is a navigation hub. It does not replace the canonical child articles that define the objective rules themselves.

## Domain Boundaries

- Objectives owns objective representation, objective control, controlling objectives, secured objectives, objective-related battlefield states, and objective-based mission interactions.
- Objectives does not own terrain features, terrain areas, visibility, cover, or other terrain-state rules, which remain owned by [[Terrain Visibility System]].
- Objectives does not own units, models, characteristics, players, or other baseline battlefield vocabulary, which remain owned by [[Core Concepts System]].
- Objectives does not own battle-shock state or its application timing, even though [[Battle-shock System]] can change objective-control outcomes.
- Objectives does not own movement procedures such as [[Normal Move]], [[Advance Move]], [[Fall-back Move]], [[Pile-in Move]], or [[Consolidation Move]], even though those moves can change which models are within range of an objective.
- [[Terrain Visibility System]] provides the terrain-area state consumed by terrain objectives.
- [[Movement System]] provides the positional state that determines whether models are within range of an objective.
- [[Battle-shock System]] modifies OC and action eligibility without absorbing ownership of objective rules.

## Navigation

- Start here when the question is about how objectives are represented, controlled, secured, or used by mission-driven battlefield tasks.
- Read [[Terrain Objectives]] when the issue is whether the objective is represented by a terrain area.
- Read [[Objectives Not Within a Terrain Area]] when the issue is an objective marker rather than a terrain area.
- Read [[Level of Control]] when the question is who controls an objective at the end of a phase or turn.
- Read [[Secured Objectives]] when the issue is persistent control after units leave objective range.
- Read [[Actions]] when the question is about mission-defined battlefield tasks that depend on objective-related eligibility and restrictions.
- Read [[Objective marker]], [[Objective Control (OC)]], or [[Terrain objective]] when the question is a support term rather than a full rule article.
- Move to [[Terrain Visibility System]] when the real issue is terrain ownership, terrain areas, visibility, or cover.
- Move to [[Movement System]] when the real issue is how a unit or model moved into or out of objective range.
- Move to [[Battle-shock System]] when the real issue is why OC changed or why an action could not start or complete.
