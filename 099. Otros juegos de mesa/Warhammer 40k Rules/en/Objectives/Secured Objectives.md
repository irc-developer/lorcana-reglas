# Secured Objectives

## Summary

Secured Objectives is the persistence rule that keeps an objective under a player's control after no units remain within range when another rule has secured it for that army.

## Definition

Some rules secure an objective for a player's army so it remains under their control even after no units remain within range.

## Rules

Secured status does not come from this article by itself. Another rule must first secure the objective for that player's army.

Once secured, the objective remains under that player's control even after no units remain within range.

The opponent takes control only once their level of control is greater at the end of a phase.

## Interactions

- [[Level of Control]] determines when the opponent has exceeded the prior securing player's control total.
- [[Objective Control (OC)]] still matters when another player attempts to take a secured objective.
- Datasheet-carried abilities may secure objectives, but those abilities remain owned by [[Datasheet System]] and their home domains rather than by Objectives.

## Restrictions

- This article does not define which rules secure objectives; it only defines what secured status means once another rule applies it.
- An opponent does not take a secured objective merely by matching the prior control total.
- Control changes only when the opponent's level of control is greater at the end of a phase.
