# Level of Control

## Summary

Level of Control is the objective-control calculation that totals each player's OC within range of an objective at the end of each phase and turn.

## Definition

At the end of each phase and turn, add together the OC of all models each player has within range of an objective to determine control.

## Rules

At the start of the battle, no objective is controlled.

To gain control of an objective, a player needs one or more models with OC 1+ within range of that objective.

For terrain objectives, a model is within range while within the relevant terrain area.

If both players have the same level of control and the objective is not secured, neither player controls it.

Units within range of an objective controlled by their player are controlling that objective if they contain one or more models with OC 1+.

## Interactions

- [[Objective Control (OC)]] is the characteristic used to produce the level-of-control total.
- [[Profiles]] is the datasheet structure that carries the OC value consumed by this calculation.
- [[Terrain Objectives]] and [[Objectives Not Within a Terrain Area]] define what it means to be within range for the two objective-representation branches.
- [[Secured Objectives]] changes whether an objective remains under a player's control after no units remain within range.
- [[Battle-shock System]] can reduce a unit's OC to -, but the control calculation itself remains owned here.
- [[Movement System]] supplies the positioning state that determines whether models are within range.

## Restrictions

- A player cannot gain control through models that do not contribute OC 1+ within range.
- If both players have the same level of control and the objective is not secured, neither player controls that objective.
- This article does not define how an objective becomes secured; that rule remains in [[Secured Objectives]].
