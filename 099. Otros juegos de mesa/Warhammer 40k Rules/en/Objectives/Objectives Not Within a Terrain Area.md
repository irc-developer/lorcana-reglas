# Objectives Not Within a Terrain Area

## Summary

Objectives Not Within a Terrain Area is the marker-based objective rule used when an objective's location point does not coincide with a terrain area.

## Definition

If an objective's location point does not coincide with a terrain area, place a 40 mm objective marker centered on that point.

## Rules

When this branch applies, the objective is represented by a 40 mm objective marker centered on the mission's objective point.

Models can move through and end moves on objective markers.

A model is within range of an objective marker while within 3" horizontally and 5" vertically.

Distances to and from an objective marker are measured to and from the closest part of it.

## Interactions

- [[Terrain Objectives]] is the default objective-representation branch, while this article defines the marker branch when no terrain area is present.
- [[Objective marker]] is the glossary support term for the physical marker used by this rule.
- [[Level of Control]] consumes this article's within-range rule when control is calculated around a marker.
- [[Movement System]] remains owner of movement procedures even though this article allows models to move through and end moves on objective markers.

## Restrictions

- This article only applies when the objective's location point does not coincide with a terrain area.
- The objective range here is measured from the marker itself, not from a terrain area.
- This article does not define movement procedures; it only defines the objective-side result that markers do not block movement endings.
