# Eligible to Fight, But Unable to Fight

## Summary

Eligible to Fight, But Unable to Fight is the fight-step pass rule that prevents melee sequencing from stalling when a player has eligible units that still cannot currently reach any enemy unit.

## Definition

If, when a player must select a unit to fight, all of that player's eligible units are more than 5 inches from all enemy units, that player can pass.

## Rules

This procedure applies only when a player is required to select a unit to fight and every eligible unit they control is still more than 5 inches from every enemy unit.

If both players pass in succession, the Fight step ends.

The Fight step also ends if one player passes when the opponent has no eligible units left.

## Interactions

- [[Fight]] provides the alternating selection sequence in which this pass option can matter.
- [[Overrun Fight]] is the adjacent special fight branch whose extra movement can change whether a later pass is still necessary.
- [[Fights First]] can change who must select next, but it does not remove this pass rule when all currently eligible units remain out of reach.

## Restrictions

- This procedure is not a general permission to skip fighting; it applies only when every eligible unit is more than 5 inches from all enemy units.
- Passing under this rule does not create attacks, movement, or new eligibility by itself.
- This article does not redefine what makes a unit eligible to fight; that remains part of [[Fight]].
