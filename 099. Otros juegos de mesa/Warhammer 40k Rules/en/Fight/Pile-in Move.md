# Pile-in Move

## Summary

Pile-in Move is the fight-owned positional procedure that lets an eligible unit move up to 3 inches to engage its pile-in targets at the start of melee resolution.

## Definition

Eligible units can move up to 3 inches to engage pile-in targets at the start of the Fight step.

## Rules

If the unit is engaged, it selects every enemy unit it is engaged with as its pile-in targets.

Otherwise, it selects one or more enemy units within 5 inches as its pile-in targets.

Models already in base contact with enemy models cannot be moved.

Each moved model must end closer to the closest pile-in target and must end engaged with that target if possible.

After the move, the unit must be engaged.

Any model that started engaged with an enemy unit must remain engaged with that enemy unit.

## Interactions

- [[Fight]] uses this procedure to position a unit before its melee attacks are resolved.
- [[Overrun Fight]] is the fight branch that explicitly adds one further use of this procedure before attacks.
- [[Consolidation Move]] is the matching fight-owned movement procedure that happens after a unit has fought.
- [[Movement System]] remains the neighbouring system for generic movement rules, but this procedure stays owned by Fight because it only exists inside melee sequencing.

## Restrictions

- The move is capped at 3 inches.
- Models in base contact with enemy models cannot be moved by this procedure.
- Each moved model must satisfy the closer-to-target requirement and the engagement requirement where possible.
- This article does not redefine generic movement pathing, advance rules, fall-back rules, or charge movement.
