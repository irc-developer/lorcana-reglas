# Overrun Fight

## Summary

Overrun Fight is the special melee branch for an eligible unit that resolves one additional pile-in move before making its attacks.

## Definition

An eligible unengaged unit fights after making one additional [[Pile-in Move]].

## Rules

This fight mode applies when the unit is unengaged, or was unengaged at the start of the Fight step but became engaged during the Fight phase.

The unit must still be eligible to fight when it is selected.

After the additional [[Pile-in Move]], the unit resolves its melee attacks through [[Making Attacks]].

Because this branch can pull previously unengaged enemy units into engagement, it can change which units later become eligible to fight.

## Interactions

- [[Fight]] provides the sequencing context that selects this branch.
- [[Pile-in Move]] is the positional procedure that makes this mode distinct from [[Normal Fight]].
- [[Making Attacks]] and [[Attack System]] remain the shared resolution pipeline once attacks start.
- [[Eligible to Fight, But Unable to Fight]] explains when a player can pass instead of selecting an eligible unit that cannot currently reach enemy units.

## Restrictions

- A unit cannot resolve this branch unless it is still eligible to fight.
- This article does not redefine melee attack steps, charge procedures, or generic movement procedures.
- The additional movement here is fight-owned because it exists only inside melee sequencing, not as a general battlefield move.
