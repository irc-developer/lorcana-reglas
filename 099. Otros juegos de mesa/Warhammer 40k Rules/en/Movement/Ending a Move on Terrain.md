# Ending a Move on Terrain

## Summary

Ending a Move on Terrain is the procedure that determines when a model can legally finish its move on ground level terrain or on an elevated surface.

## Definition

Models can end a move on ground level terrain, and some models can end a move on elevated surfaces.

## Rules

Models can end a move on ground level terrain.

To end a move on a non-ground-level surface, the model must have INFANTRY, BEASTS, SWARM, FLY, or MONSTER.

The model must be stable on that surface.

The model's base cannot overhang the surface.

Solid terrain further restricts ending a move through enclosed parts within 3" of ground level.

## Interactions

- [[Moving Units]] provides the baseline legality check that all moves must satisfy when they end.
- [[Moving Vertically]] explains how the model reaches an elevated position and how that travel is counted.
- [[Flying Models]] can help an eligible unit reach elevated positions, but it does not remove the need for a legal end position.
- [[Terrain Visibility System]] is the adjacent system map for terrain-driven constraints that matter when finishing a move.

## Restrictions

- A model cannot end on an elevated surface unless it has one of the listed permissions.
- An unstable placement or base overhang makes the end position illegal.
- This article does not authorize movement through otherwise blocked terrain sections; it only governs the legality of the end position.
