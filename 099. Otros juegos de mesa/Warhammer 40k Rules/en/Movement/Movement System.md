# Movement System

## Overview

Movement System is the authoritative MOC for the Movement domain. It maps the published concepts, procedures, glossary support, and cross-domain dependencies that together define how units change position before and after the battle begins.

## Scope

This domain covers the published movement concepts, procedures, and glossary support that govern how units change position during play and during other movement-related windows.

It helps answer questions about baseline movement branches, movement-distance interpretation, terrain-driven route constraints, and which neighbouring systems own specialised movement cases or timing windows.

## Domain Ownership

- Owner domain: Movement.
- Publication role: maintain the canonical movement articles and glossary support that define the main movement branches and route-handling rules.
- Domain system role: provide the authoritative entry point and navigation surface for the published Movement-domain footprint.

## Domain Boundaries

Movement owns:

- [[Moving Units]] and [[Different Move Characteristics]] as the published parent and supporting concept surfaces for the domain.
- [[Remain Stationary]], [[Normal Move]], [[Advance Move]], [[Fall-back Move]], [[Selecting Modes]], [[Moving Vertically]], [[Ending a Move on Terrain]], [[Moving Monsters and Vehicles]], [[Flying Models]], and [[Scout Move]] as the published movement procedures.
- [[Move Characteristic]] as glossary support for distance-related movement lookups.

Movement does not own:

- [[Battle Sequence System]] ownership of the timing windows in which movement permissions are applied.
- [[Strategic Reserves System]] ownership of reserve arrival and [[Ingress Move]] as the reserve-entry movement branch.
- [[Terrain Visibility System]] ownership of visibility, cover, and terrain-state rules beyond movement-specific route handling.
- [[Ability System]] ownership of ability-derived exceptions that may modify movement without becoming Movement-domain concepts.
- [[Core Concepts System]] ownership of foundational concepts such as [[Measuring Distances]], [[Set Up]], [[Coherency]], and [[Engagement]].

## Related Systems

- [[Core Concepts System]] provides the unit, distance, and setup baseline that movement rules consume.
- [[Ability System]] provides neighbouring rule text that can modify movement permissions or characteristics.
- [[Battle Sequence System]] provides the timing framework, while [[Movement Phase]] is the canonical phase article that tells readers when movement-domain branches are available.
- [[Terrain Visibility System]] provides the adjacent terrain-state context that constrains routes and legal end positions.
- [[Strategic Reserves System]] consumes movement through reserve arrival and owns [[Ingress Move]] as a separate cross-domain procedure.

## Navigation

- Start with [[Moving Units]] for the baseline procedure that every in-battle movement rule builds on.
- Read [[Different Move Characteristics]] and [[Move Characteristic]] when the question is about distance values rather than move permissions.
- Follow [[Remain Stationary]], [[Normal Move]], [[Advance Move]], or [[Fall-back Move]] when resolving the standard movement-phase branches.
- Use [[Selecting Modes]] when the rule presents mutually exclusive branches whose order and mandatory status matter.
- Use [[Moving Vertically]] and [[Ending a Move on Terrain]] when terrain changes route cost or legal end positions.
- Use [[Moving Monsters and Vehicles]] or [[Flying Models]] when a specialised override changes what a unit can move through.
- Use [[Scout Move]] when the question is about pre-battle repositioning rather than a movement-phase move.
- Move to [[Strategic Reserves System]] and [[Ingress Move]] when the unit is arriving from off the battlefield.
- Move to [[Movement Phase]] when the question is whether movement can currently happen.
- Move to [[Battle Sequence System]] when the question is about the wider timing spine rather than the Movement phase specifically.
