# Selecting Modes

## Summary

Selecting Modes is the procedure rule that explains how mutually exclusive movement branches are checked and chosen when a rule presents named modes.

## Definition

When a rule instructs a player to select a mode, the available modes are mutually exclusive and must be checked in the order presented.

## Rules

Check the available modes in the order the rule presents them.

If the unit does not meet the conditions of any mode, it cannot make that move.

Some applicable modes are mandatory, while others remain optional even when available.

Conditions labelled with a mode name apply only if that mode was selected.

Conditions without a mode label always apply.

## Interactions

- [[Fall-back Move]] uses named fall-back branches whose availability and mandatory status depend on this selection rule.
- [[Remain Stationary]] and several other movement procedures gain meaning from being alternative branches within the same selection context.
- This article governs selection logic, not movement distance, pathing, or terrain handling; those remain defined elsewhere in the movement domain.

## Restrictions

- Selecting Modes does not create new movement branches beyond those expressly presented by the source rule.
- The procedure does not let a player skip mandatory modes that are currently applicable.
- Failing every mode check means the move cannot be made at all.
