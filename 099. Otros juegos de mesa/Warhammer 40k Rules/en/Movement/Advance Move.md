# Advance Move

## Summary

Advance Move is the movement-phase procedure where an unengaged unit moves up to its Move Characteristic plus an advance roll, trading speed for later restrictions that turn.

## Definition

An unengaged unit on the battlefield can move up to its Move Characteristic plus an advance roll by making an advance move.

## Rules

Roll one D6 before moving to determine the advance roll.

The unit moves using the baseline procedure from [[Moving Units]].

Each model's maximum movement is its [[Move Characteristic]] plus the advance roll, using [[Different Move Characteristics]] when the unit's models do not share the same value.

After the move, the unit must still be unengaged.

Until the end of the turn, the unit cannot declare a charge or start an action unless another rule states otherwise.

## Interactions

- [[Moving Units]] provides the shared movement procedure and end-of-move legality check.
- [[Move Characteristic]] and [[Different Move Characteristics]] determine the base distance that the advance roll extends.
- [[Moving Vertically]] and [[Ending a Move on Terrain]] still apply when terrain affects the route or legal end position.
- [[Moving Monsters and Vehicles]] and [[Flying Models]] remain available specialized overrides when the unit qualifies for them.
- [[Normal Move]] is the lower-speed branch that preserves more downstream options, while [[Fall-back Move]] is the disengagement branch for engaged units.

## Restrictions

- An advance move cannot be selected by an engaged unit.
- The unit cannot finish the move engaged.
- Advancing restricts later turn actions even if the unit moved only a short distance.
