# Flying Models

## Summary

Flying Models is the movement override that lets a FLYING unit take to the skies before certain move types, changing both its maximum distance and what it can move through.

## Definition

Before making a normal, advance, fall-back, or charge move, a FLYING unit can declare that it will take to the skies.

## Rules

If the unit takes to the skies, reduce its maximum distance by 2" unless another rule changes that reduction.

While doing so, FLYING models ignore vertical distance.

While doing so, FLYING models can move through all models.

While doing so, FLYING models can move horizontally and vertically through all terrain categories.

## Interactions

- [[Moving Units]] remains the baseline movement procedure; this rule changes how an eligible unit resolves distance and pass-through permissions.
- [[Normal Move]], [[Advance Move]], and [[Fall-back Move]] are the movement-domain procedures that explicitly support taking to the skies.
- [[Moving Vertically]] is heavily modified by this rule because vertical distance stops counting while the unit is in the skies.
- [[Ending a Move on Terrain]] still matters because taking to the skies changes travel, not the requirement to end in a legal position.
- [[Moving Monsters and Vehicles]] is a separate movement override for different model classes and should not be conflated with this one.

## Restrictions

- The declaration must be made before the eligible move begins.
- Taking to the skies reduces the unit's maximum distance unless another rule says otherwise.
- This article does not extend its benefits to move types that the source rule does not list.
