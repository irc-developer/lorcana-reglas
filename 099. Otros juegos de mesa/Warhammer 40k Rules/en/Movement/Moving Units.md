# Moving Units

## Summary

Moving Units is the canonical movement article for how a unit changes position after the battle begins, including the shared constraints that still apply before any specific move mode adds its own conditions.

## Definition

When a unit moves, one or more of its models are moved one at a time in straight lines and/or rotations while the unit obeys the conditions of the selected move type.

## Rules

Models move one at a time.

Each model can move through friendly models unless another rule states otherwise.

Each model can move only through spaces its base can fit through.

A model's base cannot move through enemy models unless a more specific rule allows that movement.

A model's base cannot cross the battlefield edge.

Rotating a model does not count toward the distance it moved.

When the move ends, the unit must still satisfy [[Coherency]] and every condition that applies after moving. If it does not, the move fails and the unit returns to its starting positions.

## Interactions

- [[Different Move Characteristics]] explains how a unit resolves movement distance when its models do not share the same Move Characteristic.
- [[Remain Stationary]], [[Normal Move]], [[Advance Move]], and [[Fall-back Move]] are the primary movement-phase procedures that apply these shared rules with different entry conditions and consequences.
- [[Moving Vertically]] and [[Ending a Move on Terrain]] explain how terrain affects distance, legal paths, and legal end positions.
- [[Moving Monsters and Vehicles]] and [[Flying Models]] are specialized overrides that modify who or what a model can move through.
- [[Scout Move]] is a pre-battle move procedure that still relies on this article's baseline handling of model-by-model movement and post-move legality.

## Restrictions

- Moving Units does not replace the conditions of the selected move mode; those additional conditions still apply.
- A unit cannot complete a move that leaves it outside [[Coherency]] or fails any after-moving requirement.
- This article does not define terrain categories, reserve arrivals, embarkation, or charge/fight movement branches; it only provides the baseline movement procedure they build on.
