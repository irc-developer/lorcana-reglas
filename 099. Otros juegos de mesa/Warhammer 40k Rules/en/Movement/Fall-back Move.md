# Fall-back Move

## Summary

Fall-back Move is the disengagement procedure for an engaged unit, resolving either Ordered Retreat or Desperate Escape so that the unit ends unengaged.

## Definition

An engaged unit makes a fall-back move by moving up to its Move Characteristic in order to become unengaged.

## Rules

The unit begins the procedure engaged.

The unit moves using the baseline procedure from [[Moving Units]], modified by the applicable fall-back mode.

If the unit is not battle-shocked, Ordered Retreat is available.

If Ordered Retreat is unavailable, Desperate Escape is mandatory and requires a hazard roll for each model.

During Desperate Escape, moved models can move through enemy models.

After the move, the unit must be unengaged.

Until the end of the turn, the unit cannot shoot, declare a charge, or start an action unless another rule states otherwise.

After Desperate Escape, if the unit is not battle-shocked, it must make a battle-shock roll.

## Interactions

- [[Moving Units]] provides the baseline move procedure and the final requirement to end the move legally.
- [[Move Characteristic]] determines the maximum distance available to the unit.
- [[Selecting Modes]] explains how fall-back-specific branches are checked and why some branches become mandatory.
- [[Flying Models]] can modify what an eligible unit can move through during the fall-back move.
- [[Normal Move]] and [[Advance Move]] are the unengaged alternatives that cannot be selected while the unit is engaged.

## Restrictions

- A fall-back move must end with the unit unengaged.
- The unit loses significant downstream options for the rest of the turn unless another rule overrides those restrictions.
- This article does not publish Ordered Retreat or Desperate Escape as standalone movement-domain articles; it describes their role only as named fall-back modes.
