# Remain Stationary

## Summary

Remain Stationary is the movement-phase procedure where a unit is selected to move but no models in that unit are actually moved.

## Definition

To remain stationary is to resolve a unit's movement selection by moving no models.

## Rules

No models in the unit are moved.

Choosing to remain stationary still resolves the unit's movement selection for that phase.

Remaining stationary does not trigger rules that trigger when a unit starts or ends a move.

## Interactions

- [[Moving Units]] provides the baseline movement framework that this procedure intentionally does not advance into actual displacement.
- [[Selecting Modes]] is the generic rule pattern for move procedures that present mutually exclusive branches.
- [[Normal Move]], [[Advance Move]], and [[Fall-back Move]] are the movement-phase alternatives that actually reposition the unit.
- This procedure often matters to downstream rules that distinguish between a unit that moved and a unit that did not, even though those downstream rules are defined elsewhere.

## Restrictions

- Remaining stationary is not the same as moving zero inches; no move is started or ended.
- This procedure does not satisfy conditions that require a unit to have made a specific move type.
- This article does not define the full phase-selection procedure for every unit; it only defines the remain-stationary branch.
