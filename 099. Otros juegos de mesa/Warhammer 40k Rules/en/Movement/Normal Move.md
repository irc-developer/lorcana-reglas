# Normal Move

## Summary

Normal Move is the default battlefield movement procedure for an unengaged unit that moves up to its Move Characteristic.

## Definition

An unengaged unit on the battlefield can move up to its Move Characteristic by making a normal move.

## Rules

The unit must be on the battlefield.

The unit must be unengaged when the move is selected.

Each model moves under the baseline procedure from [[Moving Units]].

The maximum distance available to each model is determined by its [[Move Characteristic]], including [[Different Move Characteristics]] when relevant.

After the move, the unit must still be unengaged.

## Interactions

- [[Moving Units]] provides the shared model-by-model movement procedure and the final legality check.
- [[Move Characteristic]] and [[Different Move Characteristics]] determine how far each model in the unit can move.
- [[Moving Vertically]] and [[Ending a Move on Terrain]] determine how terrain changes route cost and legal end positions.
- [[Moving Monsters and Vehicles]] and [[Flying Models]] are specialized overrides that can change what the unit can move through.

## Restrictions

- A normal move cannot be selected by an engaged unit.
- The unit cannot finish the move engaged.
- This procedure does not add an advance roll, reserve arrival permission, or pre-battle timing; those belong to other rules.
