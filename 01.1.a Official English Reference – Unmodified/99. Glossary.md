## GLOSSARY

**ability**  
Special rules explaining what the card can do that differs from the game rules, such as an effect that resolves as a result of the card being played.

**action card**  
A type of card that gives its player an immediate one-time advantage. When an action card is played, its player resolves its effects and then puts it in their discard pile.

**action, game**  
When a player does something in game that’s part of a normal turn, such as draw a card, exert a character, declare a challenging character, etc., it’s a game action. A game action is singular.

**action, turn**  
One of a set of game actions with predefined processes that arrive at a specific outcome.

**activate**  
To use an activated ability of a card.

**activated ability**  
An ability that a player may use after paying its cost. An activated ability can’t be used if its cost can’t be paid. Activated abilities of items may be used during the turn the item is played.

**active player**  
The player who has started their turn. When a player ends their turn, they’re no longer the active player.

**alter a starting hand**  
When a player chooses to modify their starting hand at the beginning of the game after each player has drawn their starting hand. Each player may take any number of those cards, put them on the bottom of their deck without revealing them, draw until they have 7 cards in their hand, and reshuffle their deck. A player can’t alter their starting hand more than once in a game.

**alternate cost**  
A cost the active player can choose to pay to play a card instead of paying that card’s ink cost.

**applied static ability**  
A static ability generated from a resolved effect that applies only to cards in play at the time that effect is resolved.

**bag**  
A non-physical zone where triggered abilities wait before determining how their effects resolve. Whenever a triggered ability’s condition is met, the ability is added to the bag.

**banish / banished**  
When a character, item, or location is put from play into their player’s discard pile, either as a result of abilities or effects or when the card has damage equal to or greater than its Willpower {W}. A card put into discard this way is considered banished.

**Beginning Phase**  
The first phase of the turn, in which a player resets their cards for the turn. It has three steps: Ready, Set, and Draw.

**card type**  
The type that each card has, such as character, action, item, or location.

**challenge**  
When a player exerts one of their dry characters and chooses an exerted opposing character or an opposing location. Each character deals damage equal to their Strength {S}.

**challenging / challenged character**  
The challenging character is declared by the active player. The challenged character is the opposing character being challenged.

**challenging / challenged player**  
The challenging player declares the challenge. The challenged player owns the challenged character.

**character**  
A card with classifications and both {S} and {W}. Characters enter the Play zone and remain until banished or removed.

**classification**  
A category identifying characteristics of a card, shown to the right of the ink symbol.

**condition**  
A game state checked to be true or false.

**conditional static ability**  
A static ability that applies only if a stated condition is true or false.

**continuous static ability**  
A static ability that continuously affects cards for as long as its duration is active.

**cost**  
What is required to play a card or use an activated ability.

**damage**  
Represented by damage counters and counted against Willpower {W}.

**damage counter**  
A counter showing damage. Counters placed are not considered damage dealt.

**damaged / undamaged**  
A card with damage is damaged; one with none is undamaged.

**deck**  
A player’s set of cards used in the game. The deck is a private zone.

**delayed triggered ability**  
A triggered ability generated to resolve later.

**discard (noun / verb)**  
The discard pile is a public zone. To discard is to put a card from hand into discard.

**draw**  
To take the top card of the deck and put it into hand.

**Draw step**  
The third step of the Beginning Phase.

**dry / drying**  
Dry characters were in play at turn start. Drying characters were played this turn.

**duration**  
The length of time an ability or effect applies.

**effect**  
What happens when an action or ability resolves.

**End-of-Turn Phase**  
The third phase of the turn.

**exert / exerted**  
To turn a card sideways / a card that is sideways.

**facedown / faceup**  
Facedown cards are private; faceup cards are public.

**floating triggered ability**  
A triggered ability that exists for a duration.

**for free**  
Playing or using something without paying ink cost.

**full name**  
The combination of a card’s name and version.

**game state check**  
A rules check performed after effects resolve.

**hand**  
The zone where drawn cards are held.

**Illuminary icon**  
The icon on the back of a standard card.

**in a stack**  
Cards in a single pile with a top card and cards under it.

**in play**  
A card in the Play zone with nothing on top of it.

**ink (noun / verb)**  
The resource / putting a card into the inkwell.

**ink type**  
One of the six ink colors.

**inkwell**  
The private zone for ink cards.

**inkwell symbol {C}**  
Indicates a card can be inked.

**item**  
A card with “Item” on its classification line.

**keyword ability**  
An ability represented by a short bolded name.

**location**  
A card with “Location” on its classification line.

**lore**  
The resource players race to gain.

**Lore value  ![[imagenes/lore.svg|20]]**  
The amount of lore gained from a card.

**Main Phase**  
The second phase of the turn.

**move cost {M}**  
The cost to move a character to a location.

**multiplayer game**  
A game with three or more players.

**name**  
The card’s printed name.

**on top / under**  
Cards above or below another card.

**opponent / opposing player**  
Anyone a player is playing against.

**opposing character**  
A character controlled by an opponent.

**passing the bag**  
The process of resolving triggered abilities from the bag.

**phase**  
One of the three main parts of a turn.

**play**  
The act of playing a card from hand.

**player**  
Anyone playing the game.

**private zone / public zone**  
Zones with hidden or public information.

**quest**  
Exerting a character to gain lore.

**ready / Ready step**  
Upright cards / first step of Beginning Phase.

**reminder text**  
Italicized text that is not rules text.

**replacement effect**  
An effect that replaces another effect.

**reveal**  
Showing cards from a private zone.

**self-replacement effect**  
A replacement effect that replaces its own ability.

**sequential effect**  
An effect requiring a decision or cost to proceed.

**Set step**  
The second step of the Beginning Phase.

**shuffle**  
To randomize a deck.

**simultaneous turns**  
Turns taken at the same time in some formats.

**sing / singing**  
Exerting a character to play a song.

**song**  
A type of action card.

**stack (noun / verb)**  
Cards piled together / effects combining.

**starting hand**  
The initial 7 cards.

**starting player**  
The player who takes the first turn.

**static ability**  
An ability that is continuously active.

**Strength {S}**  
The damage a character deals.

**take / took damage**  
Being dealt or receiving damage.

**triggered ability**  
An ability added to the bag when triggered.

**turn**  
A unit consisting of three phases.

**turn action**  
An action allowed during the Main Phase.

**version**  
A descriptor differentiating cards with the same name.

**Willpower {W}**  
The amount of damage needed to banish a card.

**zone**  
A physical or non-physical gameplay space.
